# Lead Qualification

## Scoring Criteria
Each lead is scored hot/warm/cold based on these signals:

### Hot Lead (score 7-10)
- Has a website but no live chat or chatbot
- Business has reviews mentioning slow response or missed calls
- In a high-value niche (HVAC, plumber, electrician, auto repair)
- Located in a target city with high population density
- Business has been open 2+ years (established, has budget)
- Phone number present (real business, not a side hustle)

### Warm Lead (score 4-6)
- Has a website with minimal contact options
- Mid-value niche (restaurant, salon, dental, pest control)
- Smaller city or suburb
- Newer business (1-2 years old)
- Email found via Hunter.io (real contact)

### Cold Lead (score 1-3)
- No website or social presence
- Very low-value niche (low average job size)
- Saturated area with many competitors already using AI tools
- No email found, only contact form
- Chain or franchise (not a local owner-operated business)

## Qualification Questions (for calls)
1. "How do most of your customers find you?" (SEO/Google = good, word of mouth only = harder)
2. "Do you get calls or messages after hours?" (yes = perfect fit)
3. "Is there someone answering your phone full time?" (no = strong use case)
4. "What does a typical job run?" (higher average ticket = more ROI on chatbot)
5. "Have you tried any other tools to capture leads?" (previous attempt = aware of problem)

## Disqualifiers (stop pursuing)
- National chains or franchises
- B2B businesses (our chatbot is optimized for B2C local)
- Businesses that explicitly say they don't want automation
- Leads with no online presence at all
