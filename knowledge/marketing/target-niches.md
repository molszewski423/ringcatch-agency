# Target Niches

## Tier 1 — Best ROI (prioritize)

### HVAC
- Average job: $300-$3,000
- Pain point: customers call at 2am when AC breaks in summer
- Messaging: "Never miss an emergency call again"
- Conversion signal: seasonal spikes (summer heat, winter freeze)
- Best cities: Houston, Phoenix, Dallas, Atlanta, Miami

### Plumbers
- Average job: $150-$800
- Pain point: emergency calls at night/weekends, high no-answer rate
- Messaging: "Capture emergency plumbing calls 24/7"
- Good objection: they're often solo operators who can't answer while on a job
- Best cities: Chicago, Houston, Phoenix, Los Angeles

### Electricians
- Average job: $200-$1,500
- Pain point: miss calls when hands are in a panel
- Messaging: "Your chatbot answers when your hands are full"
- Best cities: all major metros

## Tier 2 — Good ROI

### Auto Repair Shops
- Average job: $100-$2,000
- Pain point: customers want to know if their car is ready, shop won't answer
- Messaging: "Let customers check job status and book service without calling"
- Note: focus on independent shops, not chains/franchises

### Restaurants (higher-end)
- Average ticket: varies
- Pain point: reservation inquiries, menu questions, event bookings
- Messaging: "Answer reservation and catering questions instantly"
- Best fit: sit-down restaurants with $20+ avg check, catering component

### Dental Offices
- Average job: $100-$5,000
- Pain point: new patient inquiries go unanswered after hours
- Messaging: "Never lose a new patient to a missed call"
- Note: HIPAA awareness — keep chatbot to scheduling/FAQ, not medical questions

## Tier 3 — Lower Priority

### Salons / Spas
- Lower average ticket ($50-$200)
- High competition from Vagaro/StyleSeat built-in chat
- Only target upscale independents

### Pest Control
- Seasonal, but good emergency call volume
- Messaging: "Capture late-night pest emergency calls"

## Cities to Target (by volume)
1. Houston, TX — large, business-friendly, strong HVAC market
2. Phoenix, AZ — extreme heat = massive HVAC demand
3. Chicago, IL — plumbing emergencies common, large market
4. Dallas, TX — growing fast, strong home services market
5. Atlanta, GA — HVAC + plumbing strong, underserved by automation
6. Los Angeles, CA — huge market, high competition but high prices too
